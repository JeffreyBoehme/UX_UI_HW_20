Save-A-Dog reimagined

A look to the future of Save-A-Dog

Save-A-Dog aims to save pets from being euthanized by rescuing and providing veterinary support to animals so they can be adopted into their forever homes. They are a Not-for-profit and rely on donations to cover vet bills, maintenance and building costs.

We want to make the saveadog website become a driver in pushing adoptions for homeless pets and donations by increasing its appeal and improving usability on the site. We hope to improve readability and usability so that the site is more streamlined and does not burden users while trying to adopt or donate.

Research Phase

Initial Assumptions and Proto Persona from User Interviews

Through our Proto Person we hypothesised our users want to know what their money goes, want to to have an easy process of adoption and donation, and want to trust the charities they interact with.

“I like to donate to the small charities because money tends to get wasted with big charities”

Interview and Heuristic evaluation results

Through Heuristic evaluation, we saw Poor visibility of system status as there is no ability to track the progress of applications, they’ll only call you if you’re successful after 10 days.

Poor error prevention and recognition & recall as donations page is external and relies on users remembering the name of the dog they want to donate to/no ability to recognize where the money will be going

Poor flexibility and efficiency of use - no search bar or ability to filter, no Login page

“The special needs section really sent me for a loop, it's not what I expected at all”

Empathy mapping and proto persona insights

During our Empathy Mapping we oncovered multiple prevalent pain points;

Spammy sites that seem like scams, not knowing where their money was going

They weren't able to find a pet last time they were searching, and they don't like working hard to contact the charities they interact with

“..the money goes to the actual cause and not to administrative waste, and the organisation is reputable”

Competitor analysis insights and notes

Some takeaways from our Competitor Analysis were the much better filtering options

big beautiful Hero sections on pages, along with uncluttered homepages and to the point navigation

Significantly more "trustworthy" feeling pages and emphasis on explaining spending

“The sites all have great filtering, clear Hero sections and their main goals right in your face”

Insights from first round of Usability Testing

Some of the key feedback we found during usability testing were that users;

Found the homepage cluttered and skipped over big blocks of text, Did not complete the Donation tasks successfully, did not understand the special needs page, found the process of donating unnecessary and cumbersome

Found it inconvenient that there was no way to tell your application status, found it inconvenient that their application details could not be saved if they applied to adopt an animal

Found the newsletter redundant and found it very hard to find, found the happy stories cute but the amount of information overwhelming and didn't understand why on the "adopted animals" page nothing could be clicked

“Majority of users found the process cumbersome and unnecessary”

Gathering UI Inspiration

We drew some of our UI Inspiration from "Food for the Hungry", "Children International", "Animal Aid" and "the RSPCA"

“..has great Hero landing picture with the vision and donate now option..”

Definition Phase

User insights

Tiffany who is a kind-hearted animal lover needs to find a dog to adopt that is active and will join her and her current dog on hikes and keep him company while she's at work. She would also like to find an organisation that she can support on an ongoing basis that is trustworthy and delegates funds responsibly because giving to animal charities and seeing the animals she supports thrive fills her with good feelings and a knowledge that she is making a positive change in the world.

Problem Statement

SaveADog was designed to help users Adopt animals and donate to support animal rescue and recovery. We have observed that users find using the SaveADog website cumbersome and confusing, which is potentially driving away adoptions and donations due to the frustrating experience and strong competitors that have a simpler and smoother experience. How might we improve the Save-A-Dog Website so that potential adopters and donaters have a seamless and engaging experience giving support and identifying pets to adopt to give the animals the best chance at a happy, healthy life?

UX Hypothesis

We believe that decluttering the homepage, improving the navigation bar to prioritise important items and adding an account option for people looking to adopt or donate to animals at Save-A-Dog, will drive users attention to the website and enable users to keep track of their donation or adoption progress, create loyalty and increase the frequency of donations.

Story Board

1. Tiffany's dog needs company while she's at work

Tiffany's dog Rufus is lonely while she's at work and needs some company. His pent up energy means that every time she comes home, something else is destroyed.

2. Tiffany decides to Adopt with Save-A-Dog

Tiffany would prefer to adopt rather than buy a puppy from a breeder as she is always seeking out ways to help give animals a better life. Tiffany heard about Save-A-Dog from a friend and clearly sees their values and no kill policy.

3. Tiffany filters results to find a dog to suit her needs

Tiffany easily filters the adoptable dogs to find one that is active and good with other dogs and applies to adopt 'Sanders' (Rufus approves)

4. Tiffany Creates an Account and Applies to Adopt

Tiffany creates an account and fills out the adoption form. Once she submits, the adoption process is updated on her account and she can clearly see the progress of her application and what steps are required next.

5. Tiffany's Adoption is Approved!

Tiffany gets a notification that her application has been approved and she can make an appointment to come pick up Sanders, Tiffany and Rufus head to the shelter and he and Sanders immediately bond.

6. Tiffany wants to continue support to Save-A-Dog

Rufus is so tired from playing with Sanders all day, he no longer eats the couch and Sanders is happy to finally have a loving home. Tiffany really enjoyed her experience with Save-A-Dog and can clearly see that her values align from their website. She decides to start ongoing monthly donations so that she knows she's helping as many animals as possible.

Feature Prioritisation Matrix

Our features that came from our Feature Prioritisation Matrix were;

To improve the Sponsor a Dog portion of the site, giving users more control over their donations and sponsorships, improving sponsorship listing and filtering.

To improve the Filtering system, Filtering System - age, background, breed, temperament, Maybe Icons for good with kids, good with other animals, no kids etc.

To create a Login system, Include donation tracking and allow users to pause donations, adoption statuses and the ability to save adoption details, show current sponsorships and how funds are being spent, give users transaction receipts,

Testing Phase

Testing Completed

During our testing phase, we completed Initial usability tests, Guerilla tests at both the lofi and hi fi stages, completed testing on iterated wireframe and completed feedback analysis.

User Testing Results

Our testing results spoke for themselves, we saw vast improvements in donation, adoption and information tasks and users responded well to our account additions.

Iterations from Testing

1. Emphasizing Values

Users did not engage with our original prototype but we found they engaged well with the redesign

2. Donations

In our first prototype we found that users loved the straightforwardness but wanted to know where their money was going. In our iteration we focused on breaking down donation paths and providing clear information upfront without too much to overload our users, this information is based on the radio button selected.

3. Sponsor a Pet

Our initial design of the sponsored pets card in the account was well received but users preferred to have amount donated as part of sponsorship card which we completed in our iteration users loved the ability to pause payments at any time without having to cancel, they said this allowed them a flexibility to manage their payments they are not given elsewhere.

4. Correcting Choice Overload

Through our iteration when sponsoring a pet in the Donations page, we also decided to show 5 pets chosen by Save-A-Dog that were in the most need so that the user, while still providing the option to find their own pet to sponsor so that didn’t have to think and reduce the overwhelming amount of choice from the special needs page.
